About This Dataset

The COMPAS (Correctional Offender Management Profiling for Alternative Sanctions) dataset became one of the most important datasets in algorithmic fairness research following ProPublica's groundbreaking 2016 investigation, "Machine Bias." It has since been cited in over 1,200 academic papers and remains a standard benchmark for fairness research.

ProPublica obtained risk scores for over 7,000 defendants arrested in Broward County, Florida during 2013-2014 through a public records request. They then tracked which defendants were charged with new crimes over the next two years to evaluate the accuracy and fairness of the COMPAS algorithm developed by Northpointe (now Equivant).

Key Findings from ProPublica's Analysis

The investigation revealed significant racial disparities in the algorithm's error rates, fundamentally changing how we think about fairness in automated decision-making:

  • Black defendants were almost twice as likely as white defendants to be labeled a higher risk but not actually re-offend (false positive rate disparity)
  • White defendants who did re-offend were much more likely than Black defendants to have been labeled lower risk (false negative rate disparity)
  • The algorithm's overall accuracy was only about 60%, roughly equivalent to predictions made by people with no criminal justice expertise
  • Race was not used as an input variable, yet the disparities persisted through proxy variables

Known Biases and Limitations

⚠️ Important Considerations

  • Racial Disparities in Error Rates: Black defendants scored higher risk at nearly twice the rate of white defendants who did not reoffend
  • Asymmetric False Negatives: White defendants who did reoffend were more frequently scored lower risk than Black defendants who did not
  • Age-Race Correlation: Age is a strong predictor and correlates with race in this dataset, creating proxy discrimination pathways
  • Single Jurisdiction: Data is from Broward County, Florida only and may not generalize to other jurisdictions or demographics
  • Limited Follow-up: Two-year recidivism window may undercount actual reoffending, particularly for serious crimes with longer time horizons
  • Arrest as Proxy: Uses re-arrest as a proxy for re-offending, which conflates criminal behavior with policing patterns and creates measurement bias
  • Proprietary Algorithm: The COMPAS scoring methodology is proprietary and not fully disclosed by Northpointe (now Equivant), limiting reproducibility

Recommended Uses

  • Fairness metric benchmarking and comparison
  • Bias auditing methodology development
  • Educational purposes in algorithmic fairness courses
  • Evaluating and comparing debiasing algorithms
  • Studying trade-offs between different fairness definitions

Not Recommended For

  • Production recidivism prediction systems
  • Generalizing to jurisdictions outside the original study context
  • Drawing conclusions about current criminal justice practices
  • Any real-world deployment affecting individuals' liberty
